Research

Research areas

We have three active areas of research: Mass Cytometry, Polymer Chemistry in Latex Films, and Cellulose Nanocrystals (CNCs). Though they span different scopes, much of our work involves inter-project collaboration between lab members. Scroll down to see some interactive examples of our work!

Mass Cytometry

Synthesizing and functionalizing polymers and nanoparticles as "mass tags" for high-dimensional bioanalysis. These mass tags are loaded with heavy metals that are uncommon in vivo, and functionalized with antibodies specific to the target antigen. Ionizing a cell sample treated with mass tags and passage through a time-of-flight mass cytometry device reveals the relative expression of many proteins at once, giving us a glimpse into the cell's activity.

Polymer Chemistry in Latex Films

Polymer Chemistry in Latex Films

Working closely with scientists at BASF, we are studying the mechanisms behind how polymers diffuse across interfaces in latex films. We do this through methods we developed based on Fluorescence Resonance Energy Transfer (FRET), and use this information to understand how various additives and chemical properties affect film formation.

Cellulose Nanocrystals (CNCs)

Made out of the most bioabundant polymer in the world, cellulose nanocrystals are rod-shaped nanoparticles that are exceptionally biocompatible, with low-toxicity. We are investigating how CNCs can be surface-functionalized as a vehicle for both drug delivery and imaging in cancer.
