# Reference: BBML — Biological and Biomimetic Materials Laboratory (Ali Miserez, NTU)

A visually dramatic academic lab site — full-bleed macro photography (squid,
biological specimens) fills the entire hero viewport behind bold centered
white type. Useful for typographic scale and confidence; not for imagery
strategy, since the Winnik brief explicitly wants the hero led by a
molecular/hairline diagram, not a large photo.

## What to borrow

- **Type scale and confidence**: the lab acronym ("BBML") is set enormous,
  bold, centered, with a shorter subtitle line beneath it in a smaller
  bold weight — a clean two-tier hero headline hierarchy. Good reference
  for how large the Winnik hero thesis line could go, and for pairing a
  short bold line with a longer descriptive line beneath it.
- **Minimal nav with dropdowns**: small, restrained top nav (Home,
  Research, People, News, Careers, Contact), understated weight, with
  dropdown carets on a couple of items (Research, People) for sub-pages —
  relevant if the Winnik site ends up needing sub-pages under Research or
  Publications rather than pure single-page anchors.
- **Full-bleed edge-to-edge hero** with no visible container/margin —
  confirms that a hero doesn't need to be boxed in to read as
  intentional; could inform how large the Winnik hairline molecular
  diagram is allowed to run (edge-to-edge rather than contained).

## What NOT to carry over

- The large full-bleed photographic hero itself — the Winnik brief is
  explicit that the hero should lead with the hairline molecular
  structure and thesis line, "not the lab photo front-and-center." This
  site does the opposite of that instruction.
- Full color imagery throughout — off-brief for the strict monochrome
  requirement.
- Overall this is the weakest structural match of the four references;
  treat it as a type-scale/confidence reference only, not a layout guide.
