# Reference: angelaricciardi.com (via SiteInspire, "Minimal" category)

A real artist/photographer's personal site. Strong structural match for
Winnik's restraint-first aesthetic — note this is the artist's own actual
photography, so borrow the *layout/interaction pattern* only, never the
artwork or any of her copy.

## What to borrow

- **Single-item archive viewer, not a scrolling grid**: the whole page is
  one large centered image/item at a time, framed with generous
  off-white/cream padding around it (like a mat around a print). No
  competing content on screen — one thing gets full attention.
- **Three-part minimal top bar doing all the wayfinding**: site name (top
  left) · a plain-text description of what you're browsing, e.g.
  "Projects, Information, Archive" (top center) · a numbered position
  indicator like "001 of 029" (top right). No icons, no buttons — just
  three small mono-ish text labels and a hairline rule underneath. This
  is a much quieter alternative to a conventional nav bar and reads as
  editorial/archival rather than "app UI."
- **Bottom filmstrip for direct navigation**: a horizontal strip of small
  thumbnails along the bottom edge, current item highlighted, letting you
  jump directly to any item without paging through sequentially.
- **Where this could apply on Winnik**: this numbered-archive-viewer
  pattern maps very well onto a Publications page (each paper as "001 of
  N", prev/next + jump-to-any via a thumbnail/number strip) or a
  single-research-project deep-dive view, rather than a conventional
  list. Worth considering once those pages get built out for real.

## What NOT to carry over

- Any of the actual photography, artist statement text, or specific
  imagery — this is a working artist's own portfolio content.
- The dark, high-contrast art-photography mood (deep red/black) — Winnik
  stays on `--paper`/`--ink`, no moody color fields.
