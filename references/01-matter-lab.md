# Reference: The Matter Lab (matter.toronto.edu) — PRIMARY REFERENCE

Alán Aspuru-Guzik's chemistry/quantum computing lab at U of T. This is the
user's favourite of the four references. It is a Webflow build, colorful
(not monochrome), so **borrow structure and confidence, not the palette** —
translate every color cue below into black/white/gray per the Winnik brief.

## What to borrow

- **Hero**: full-bleed dark background (theirs is a deep forest green,
  `#16241f`-ish, with a very subtle repeating dot-grid texture behind
  everything — barely visible, adds depth without noise). Large group photo
  set in a bordered rectangle on the left, oversized bold condensed
  display headline ("OUR MISSION") to the right at ~72-90px. Body copy is
  short — 3-4 sentences — set in a plain, clean sans at normal weight,
  good line height. For Winnik: use `--ink` in place of the dark green,
  keep the same confident scale and restraint (one short mission
  statement, not a wall of text).
- **Nav**: slim, single row, wordmark + logo mark at left (theirs is a
  small dot-grid icon), all links right-aligned in a single line, no
  hamburger until mobile. Text weight is medium/bold, sentence case,
  generous letter-spacing. This maps directly onto the Winnik bracket-*n*
  wordmark + right-aligned section links.
- **Section identity via icons, not photos**: each research area gets its
  own small circular hairline icon (skeletal molecule / abstract line
  art) inside a dotted circular ring, colored differently per topic. This
  is the single most relevant idea for Winnik: swap "color-coded" for
  "consistent ink-on-paper hairline icons," one per research area (MCPs,
  MSNs, CNCs, etc.), each in its own dotted circular ring. Keep the ring +
  icon treatment; drop the color-per-topic.
- **Circular "badge" as a section divider**: a large solid-fill circle
  containing bold centered text ("RECENT NEWS & PUBLICATIONS") is used to
  break up the page between sections. Translate as a large `--ink`-filled
  circle with `--paper` text, used sparingly as one section marker — fits
  the brief's molecular-diagram theme (reads like an atom).
- **People page**: circular headshot photos ringed by a dotted circular
  border (like a halo / orbit), grouped under a bold condensed all-caps
  section headline ("OUR PRINCIPAL INVESTIGATORS"). Pill-shaped nav
  buttons ("Our Postdocs," "Our Graduate Students," "Alumni") to jump
  between sub-groups. For Winnik: use the dotted-halo photo ring (subs in
  nicely for "grayscale photo, consistent treatment") and consider
  pill-shaped anchor buttons if the member list needs sub-grouping (PI /
  students / postdocs), though the group is small enough this may be
  unnecessary.
- **Alternating section bands**: sections alternate between the dark
  background and an off-white/light-gray band — gives strong visual
  rhythm scrolling down. Matches the Winnik brief's `--paper` /
  `--paper-2` alternating bands directly.
- **Typography pairing**: heavy condensed grotesk for display headlines
  (all-caps, tight tracking, almost poster-like) against a plain
  workhorse sans for body and nav. This maps well to Space Grotesk
  (display) + Inter (body) from the brief — lean into a bolder/heavier
  display weight than you might default to.

## What NOT to carry over

- The multi-hue color-per-topic system (red/yellow/magenta/mint) — the
  Winnik brief is strict monochrome, no accent color at all.
- Nothing else stands out as off-brief; this site is otherwise a strong
  structural match.
