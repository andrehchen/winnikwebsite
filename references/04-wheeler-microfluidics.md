# Reference: Wheeler Microfluidics Laboratory (microfluidics.utoronto.ca)

Aaron Wheeler's lab-on-a-chip group at U of T — included as a **negative
reference**: this is close to what the current Winnik site probably looks
and feels like (dated academic-lab template), and is useful mainly as a
list of tells to actively avoid.

## What NOT to carry over (the main value of this reference)

- Navy-blue-and-light-blue corporate palette with a stock-photo-style hero
  (droplets on a chip) — generic and dated; exactly the kind of default
  the Winnik brief is trying to escape.
- Script/italic accent font mixed with a bold sans logo wordmark ("Welcome
  to the" in italic script above "WHEELER Laboratory" in bold blue) —
  mismatched type pairing, reads as un-intentional rather than a
  deliberate two-voice system. The Winnik brief's three-role type system
  (display/body/mono) with one deliberate pairing is a direct improvement
  on this.
- "Play Video" button with a default rounded-pill outline style, drop
  shadows on the header bar — small dated UI details, avoid drop shadows
  entirely per the brief.
- Dense, left-aligned "About Us" paragraph immediately below the fold with
  no visual breathing room — the Winnik brief calls for generous line
  height and intentional whitespace instead.

## Anything worth borrowing

- Little to nothing structurally. The one transferable idea: the small
  logo mark in the top-left corner (a simple geometric "W" in a bordered
  square) shows a compact, literal wordmark-as-icon can work as a nav
  anchor — same idea as the Winnik bracket-*n* lockup, executed more
  plainly. Worth noting only as confirmation the Winnik approach
  (typographic lockup instead of a picture-logo) is the right call.
