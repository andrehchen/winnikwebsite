# Reference: Li Laboratory @ U of T — Team page (li-bowen.com/team)

Bowen Li's pharmaceutics/RNA lab, Squarespace-built. A much simpler,
editorial-list approach to a people page — useful as a fallback pattern if
the dotted-halo grid from the Matter Lab reference (see
[[01-matter-lab]]) feels like too much production for a group this size.

## What to borrow

- **Team page structure**: full-width banner photo of the whole group at
  the top of the page (real photo, not illustration) with a large white
  headline ("Meet Our Team") overlaid centered on it. Directly usable for
  Winnik's People section hero if the group has a good group photo — crop
  it wide, overlay the section title in `--paper` text with sufficient
  contrast (may need a dark scrim/gradient at the bottom of the photo
  where the Winnik `--ink` text would sit, or keep the overlay text at the
  top where the sky/background is more open).
- **List layout, not a grid**: each member is a horizontal row — square-ish
  portrait photo on the left, name (bold, larger, colored) + role + email
  + affiliations stacked on the right, hairline rule dividing each row
  from the next. This is a legitimate, simpler alternative to a photo
  grid for the Winnik People section, especially useful for the PI's
  fuller bio row vs. the shorter member rows below it — the format
  naturally accommodates rows of different lengths without breaking a
  grid.
- **Expand/collapse chevron**: each row has a small chevron to expand
  additional detail (full bio, more affiliations) without leaving the
  page. Could work well for Mitch Winnik's long PI bio (see BRIEF.md §6)
  — show 2-3 condensed paragraphs by default with a "read more" affordance
  instead of always showing the full text, per the brief's own suggestion
  to condense if it reads heavy.
- **Nav bar**: plain text links, all-caps, small size, evenly spaced,
  single row, with a search icon at the far right. Clean and unfussy —
  consistent with the Winnik brief's sticky top nav idea.

## What NOT to carry over

- Photos are in natural color, not grayscale — Winnik brief calls for
  unified grayscale treatment on all member photos.
- The name-in-blue-link-color styling — no accent color in the Winnik
  palette; would need to render names in `--ink` (bold weight for
  emphasis instead of color).
- Otherwise fairly generic/dated Squarespace chrome (default shadows,
  default type) — take the *structure* (banner + list rows + chevron),
  not the visual execution.
