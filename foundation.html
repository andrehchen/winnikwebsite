Mass cytometry is a high-throughput multiparameter bioanalytical technique for studying biomarker expression on individual cells. Antibodies or other reagents are used to label the cells with isotopes of heavy atoms (mass tags) that can then be detected by atomic mass spectrometry with single mass resolution.
